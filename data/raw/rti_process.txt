RIGHT TO INFORMATION (RTI) ACT

What is RTI:
- Legal right to access government information
- Enacted in 2005
- Empowers citizens to ask questions to government offices
- Government must reply within 30 days

When to Use RTI:
- Application status not updating
- Delayed government services
- Corruption or misconduct suspicion
- Information about government schemes
- Reasons for rejection not clear
- Complaint not resolved

Who Can File RTI:
- Any Indian citizen
- No age limit
- No educational qualification required
- Can be filed individually or through organization

Information You Can Seek:
- Status of your pending application
- Reason for delay in service
- Copies of documents in government records
- Rules and procedures of schemes
- Details of beneficiaries (in some cases)
- Action taken on complaints

Information You CANNOT Seek:
- Personal information of third parties
- Cabinet discussions
- Information affecting national security
- Trade secrets
- Court records (file directly in court)

How to File RTI:
Step 1: Identify the Department
- Find which office handles your query
- Example: Education Dept for scholarships

Step 2: Write RTI Application
Format:
---
To,
The Public Information Officer (PIO)
[Department Name]
[Address]

Subject: RTI Application under Section 6(1)

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am a citizen of India and request the following information under RTI Act 2005:

1. [Your specific question]
2. [Second question if any]

I am enclosing ₹10 as application fee via IPO/DD/Online.

Name: [Your name]
Address: [Complete address]
Mobile: [Number]
Date: [Date]
---

Step 3: Submit Application
Methods:
- Online: Through RTI portal (rtionline.gov.in)
- Offline: By hand or post to PIO office
- Pay ₹10 fee (IPO/DD for postal, online payment for portal)

Step 4: Receive Acknowledgement
- Note application number
- PIO has 30 days to respond
- If life/liberty issue: 48 hours

Step 5: Response Analysis
- If satisfied: Case closed
- If not satisfied: File first appeal
- If no response: File first appeal

Fee Structure:
- Application fee: ₹10
- First appeal: No fee
- Second appeal: No fee
- Additional pages: ₹2 per page
- BPL applicants: Free

Common Mistakes to Avoid:
- Asking vague questions ("Give all information about...")
- Not specifying time period
- Filing to wrong department
- Not paying fee
- Asking for opinions (ask facts only)
- Writing emotional complaints (be factual)

Sample RTI Questions:
For Scholarship:
"What is the current status of my scholarship application number [XXXX] submitted on [date]? If rejected, provide reasons with relevant rule citations."

For Certificate Delay:
"My income certificate application [number] was submitted on [date]. What is the current status? If under verification, which officer is assigned and expected completion date?"

For Grievance:
"I filed complaint number [XXXX] on [date] regarding [issue]. What action has been taken? Please provide copies of all correspondence and action taken reports."

First Appeal Process:
When to File:
- No response in 30 days
- Response is incomplete
- Fee demanded is unreasonable
- Information denied wrongly

How to File:
- Write to First Appellate Authority (FAA)
- Mention original RTI details
- Explain why you're dissatisfied
- Submit within 30 days of response (or 30 days after due date if no response)
- No fee required

Second Appeal:
- File with State/Central Information Commission
- Within 90 days of First Appeal order
- Can be done online through commission website
- Commission's decision is final (can challenge in High Court)

Timeline:
- RTI response: 30 days
- First appeal decision: 30-45 days
- Second appeal hearing: 3-6 months

Tips for Success:
- Be specific and clear
- One issue per RTI (don't club multiple issues)
- Keep proof of submission
- Follow up if no response
- Quote specific sections if possible
- Maintain professional tone
